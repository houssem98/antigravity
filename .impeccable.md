## Design Context

### Users
Retail crypto traders and professional desks. Same product, two audiences — the density must satisfy a pro while the affordances don't alienate a retail user. Used at a desk during active sessions AND on phone for quick checks. Primary jobs: **deep research on one asset** and **portfolio monitoring**.

### Brand Personality
Three words: **surgical, dense, unimpressed**. Not "modern." Not "elegant." This product is a tool that knows its job and doesn't flatter the user. Confidence comes from information-per-pixel, not from gloss.

### Aesthetic Direction

**Feel like:** Bloomberg Terminal — high info density, mechanical typography, directional color only where it carries signal, no decoration for decoration's sake. Command-center, not lifestyle app.

**Must not feel like:** CoinMarketCap and its lineage — gradient headers, emoji, rounded cartoon cards, purple-blue glow accents, "retail-fun" padding.

**Theme:** Dark primary (active trading default); light available (paper-cream, not white) for bright-room reading. Theme toggle respected.

**Palette:** Warm-tinted graphite neutrals (OKLCH, hue ~55-75), signature **amber** accent (single rare use — CTAs, live indicators, nothing else), directional muted green/red for up/down only. No cyan. No purple→blue. No neon. No glow.

**Typography:** `Archivo` (UI, body) + `Archivo Narrow` (display, labels) + `Martian Mono` (every number, every tabular column — tabular-nums locked). No Inter, no IBM Plex, no Space Grotesk.

**Density:** Bloomberg — 12-13px data type, compact rows, tight radii (2-6px, never 12+), multi-panel tiling, labels in small uppercase.

### Design Principles

1. **Signal over chrome.** Every pixel must carry data or structure. If it decorates, it goes. No glassmorphism, no noise overlays, no breathing glows, no gradient text, no side-stripe borders, no decorative sparklines.
2. **Numbers are first-class.** Monospace, tabular-nums, fixed column widths, aligned decimals. A column of prices should read like a spreadsheet, not prose.
3. **Directional color is sacred.** Green/red only mean up/down. Never a "success toast" green or a "delete button" red. Amber is the only brand accent and it's rare — overuse kills signal.
4. **Rhythm through density.** Varied spacing within a dense grid: tight groupings for related rows, generous separation for panel boundaries. Never uniform padding.
5. **Pro shortcuts, retail-safe defaults.** Keyboard-navigable everything. Hover reveals secondary actions. Nothing retail-critical hidden behind pro-only affordances.
